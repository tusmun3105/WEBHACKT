What is Online Scamming?

An online scam is a dishonest scheme carried out over the internet. The goal is to defraud potential victims and generate financial gain for the perpetrator. Most scammers are after your bank account details, though some might also install malware on your computer.





How do I know if I am being scammed online?




  • Emails or messages from unfamiliar numbers of email addresses.

  • Urgent requests.

  • Wire transfer requests.

  • Asking you for personal information.

  • Sweepstake or prize giveaways.

Protecting yourself from online scams





  • Make sure any websites you use are secure. Reliable websites starts with HTTPS, not HTTP.
  • Once the page has loaded, make sure the website address that you have entered hasn’t changed to a slightly different spelling.
  • Use safe and secure WiFi connections.
  • Keep virus protection software up-to-date.
  • Make sure all accounts have a strong password. Don’t use the same password for multiple accounts and change them regularly.

Types of Online Scams

Tech Support Scams



Tech support scams typically involve unsolicited calls or pop-up messages claiming to be from legitimate tech support services. Scammers convince individuals that their computers have issues and request remote access or payment for unnecessary services, preying on the victims' lack of technical knowledge for financial gain.

Romance Scams


Romance scams involve building fake online relationships to exploit individuals emotionally and financially. Scammers create compelling personas, gain their victims' trust, and then request money for various reasons, such as medical emergencies or travel expenses, leaving victims both heartbroken and financially devastated.


Lottery or Prize Scams


Victims receive notifications claiming they have won a lottery, prize, or sweepstakes, but in order to claim the winnings, they are required to pay fees or provide personal information. In reality, there is no legitimate prize, and the scammers aim to exploit the victims financially.


Charity Scams



Scammers take advantage of people's generosity by posing as charitable organizations, especially during times of natural disasters or crises. They request donations, but the funds end up in the hands of fraudsters rather than supporting legitimate causes.

Summary

Online scamming involves deceptive practices carried out over the internet to exploit individuals, businesses, or organizations for financial gain or other malicious purposes. Scammers employ various tactics, including phishing, identity theft, online shopping fraud, tech support scams, lottery or prize hoaxes, romance scams, and investment fraud. These schemes often rely on the manipulation of trust, ignorance, or vulnerabilities of targets. To protect against online scams, individuals need to exercise caution when sharing personal information, verify the legitimacy of online entities, use secure passwords, and stay informed about emerging scam tactics. While law enforcement and cybersecurity efforts aim to combat online scams, public awareness and education are crucial in preventing individuals from falling victim to these deceptive practices.
